Pixel

font-absolute
Pros

Better control and predictability across all browsers. Designers still think in pixels. Consistent rendering.

Cons

Does not scale for smaller devices or for users who are visually-impaired. Becoming more difficult to define, especially in context of reference points and very high resolution screens.

Em

font-relative
Pros

Responsive and scalable for smaller devices and accessible for the visually-impaired. Usage: the body tag's font-size is typically set to 62.5% (so that 1em = 10px).

Cons

Causes odd compounding issue with child elements, unless you reset the em for the child element. Not as predictable across browsers and devices.

Rem

font-relative CSS3
Pros

The rem ("root em") unit has the same Pros as the em, but doesn't have the compounding issue with child elements. The html tag's font-size is typically set to 62.5% (so that 1rem = 10px).

Cons

Browser support isn't as widespread as em and px. If you use rem, you will probably need some kind of pixel back-up for those browsers. Not as predictable across browsers and devices.
